Welcome to our tribute to some of the most influential Black leaders who have made lasting impacts on the world. These individuals have shaped history, fought for justice, and inspired generations to come. Explore their stories and learn how they paved the way for future leaders.

Key Moments in Black History

  • 1863: Emancipation Proclamation signed by Abraham Lincoln.
  • 1955: Rosa Parks sparks the Montgomery Bus Boycott.
  • 1963: Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. delivers "I Have a Dream" speech.
  • 1994: Nelson Mandela becomes President of South Africa.

Did You Know?

  • The first African American woman to travel to space was Dr. Mae Jemison in 1992.
  • Harriet Tubman was the first woman to lead an armed military operation in the U.S.
  • Dr. Shirley Jackson conducted breakthrough scientific research that led to inventions like the touch-tone phone.
